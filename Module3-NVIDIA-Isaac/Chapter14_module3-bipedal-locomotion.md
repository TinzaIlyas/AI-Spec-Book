# Module 3: Bipedal Locomotion

## Overview

Humanoid robots ke liye bipedal locomotion ek challenging problem hai. Ye chapter humanoid walking principles, balance maintenance, aur dynamic stability ke concepts ko cover karega.

## Fundamentals of Bipedal Walking

Bipedal locomotion human-like walking movement hai jisme robot sirf do legs ka istemal karke chalta hai. Ye statically unstable movement hai jisme continuous balance adjustment zaroori hoti hai.

### Key Principles

- **Zero Moment Point (ZMP)**: Robot ke balance maintain karne ke liye critical concept
- **Center of Mass (CoM)**: Position control ke liye important
- **Dynamic Balance**: Movement ke dauran stability maintain karna
- **Foot Placement Strategy**: Step timing aur placement

## Walking Patterns

Different walking patterns humanoid robots ke liye istemal kiye jaate hain:

### Static Walking
- Slow lekin stable
- Continuous ground contact
- High energy consumption

### Dynamic Walking
- Faster aur efficient
- Periodic ground contact
- More complex control

### Passive Dynamic Walking
- Gravity-assisted movement
- Low energy consumption
- Limited control flexibility

## Control Strategies

### Inverted Pendulum Model
Simple model for balance control:

- Linear inverted pendulum
- Capture point theory
- Preview control

### Whole-Body Control
Advanced approach considering:

- Joint coordination
- Torque distribution
- External disturbances

## Stability Analysis

Stability maintain karna critical hai humanoid locomotion ke liye:

- Lyapunov stability
- Phase portraits
- Basin of attraction

## Practical Considerations

Real-world implementation mein challenges hote hain:

- Surface irregularities
- External disturbances
- Actuator limitations
- Sensor noise
